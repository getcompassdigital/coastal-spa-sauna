The site review

Five questions we answer early.

The right answer varies by property and model. A site conversation turns general requirements into a useful plan.

01

Can it reach the site?

We measure gates, turns, overhead clearances and the final approach. Crane access is considered only when ground delivery is not practical.

02

Can the surface carry it?

A filled spa can weigh several thousand pounds. Concrete, reinforced decks and engineered pads must be evaluated for the selected model.

03

Where will water go?

Drainage, snow, roof runoff and service access matter around both hot tubs and outdoor saunas.

04

How will it be powered?

Most larger spas and traditional saunas need dedicated 240V service installed by a qualified electrician.

05

How will it feel to use?

Privacy, wind, the view, the path from the house and cover movement often matter more than a few extra feet of distance.

06

Can it be serviced?

Equipment panels and important components need practical clearance after landscaping and surrounding finishes are complete.

Planning questions

Useful before the site visit.

What photos should I take?

Photograph the intended position, the complete path from the street, the narrowest gate or turn, nearby electrical service and the relationship to the house.

Do I need a concrete pad?

Not in every case. The correct foundation depends on the model, soil, drainage and surrounding construction. It must be level and engineered for the filled weight.

Can a spa sit on an existing deck?

Only after a qualified professional confirms that the structure can support the concentrated filled load and occupants.

Who handles the electrical work?

A licensed electrician should install required circuits, disconnects and protection. We provide the selected model’s electrical requirements and coordinate timing.

How much service clearance is needed?

Requirements vary by model. We identify equipment access before permanent decking, walls or planting are finalized.
